Rick Broider
Systems Architect · Community Builder · Author

Working at the intersection of regenerative culture, conscious technology, and organizational design.

"I did not write this to explain collapse from the outside. I wrote it because I had been inside enough of these situations to see the same patterns ending different projects in different places, and I needed the architecture to exist."

Rick has spent years working inside regenerative communities, intentional organizations, and New Earth projects across multiple continents. He is also known in some of those communities by his spiritual name, Omja Brhad. This work reflects both: the systems architect who needs frameworks that survive real humans, and the practitioner who understands why people build these projects in the first place.

He is based in Costa Rica, where he works with founders, builders, and community leaders navigating the specific challenges that come with building something that is meant to matter.

What he has observed

After years of working inside these spaces, one pattern became undeniable: the same seven structural dynamics account for most of the ways regenerative projects collapse. Not bad intentions. Not lack of effort. Structural patterns that, once named, become workable.

That observation became the framework behind Bridging Earth and Kanaria and the Navigation Legend.
